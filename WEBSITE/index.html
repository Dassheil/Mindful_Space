Bukidnon offers various tranquil spaces where people can relax and reconnect with nature or themselves. These areas not only provide a break from daily stress but also contribute to mental health by reducing anxiety, improving mood, and fostering mindfulness. Here are some notable locations:

1. Dahilayan Gardens and Resort (Manolo Fortich): The serene environment, cool climate, and lush greenery provide a relaxing atmosphere that reduces stress and enhances mental clarity by lowering cortisol levels.

2. Mt. Kitanglad Range Natural Park: Trekking or enjoying the pristine beauty of this UNESCO park encourages physical activity and fosters a sense of accomplishment, helping to combat depression and boost mental health.

3. Lake Apo (Valencia City): This crater lake, surrounded by lush vegetation, offers a serene setting where activities like boating, picnicking, or admiring the calm waters help reduce stress and soothe the mind.

4. Impasugong Communal Ranch (Impasugong): Known as the home of Bukidnon's cowboy culture, its wide-open fields, rolling hills, and grazing animals create a peaceful, stress-free environment that feels like an escape into another world.

Information and Resources

The World Health Organization (WHO) estimates that around 280 million people worldwide have depression, which is approximately 3.8% of the global population.

This includes:

5% of adults

5.7% of adults over 60 years old

Depression Statistics Everyone Should Knows:

Depression is more common in women than men, affecting about 50% more women than men. However, the actual number of people with depression may be higher because many people don't seek professional help.

The WHO estimates that more than 75% of people in low-income or middle-income countries don't receive treatment for depression.Depression can affect people of all ages and from all walks of life, but some factors can increase the risk of developing it, including:

◯ Poverty

◯ Unemployment

◯ Death of a loved one or a relationship break-up

◯ Physical illness

◯ Problems caused by alcohol and drug use

Given the alarming statistics and the fact that many individuals, particularly in low-income countries, don't receive the support they need, it's clear that mental health awareness and accessibility are more important than ever. The creation of Mindful Minds is our response to this urgent need. By providing a safe, supportive space, we aim to raise awareness, reduce stigma, and offer resources to those affected by depression and mental health challenges. Our mission is to ensure that no one feels alone in their struggles, empowering individuals to seek help, connect, and heal. We are here to remind everyone that mental wellness is a journey, and it's okay to take that journey one step at a time.
